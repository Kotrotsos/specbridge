## The Problem: Quantified

### Software Project Failure Statistics

| Metric | Value | Implication |
|--------|-------|-------------|
| Projects traced to poor requirements | **70%** | Requirements is THE problem |
| Dev time on requirements clarification | **30-40%** | Massive productivity drain |
| Annual waste on failed projects | **$260B** | Huge cost savings opportunity |
| Project success rate | **39%** | Most projects fail or are challenged |
| Projects canceled before completion | **31.1%** | 1 in 3 never ship |
| Features never used | **45%** | Misalignment = wasted effort |
| Large IT projects over budget | **45%** | Predictable overruns |
| Value delivered vs predicted | **56% less** | Expectations consistently missed |

### Requirements-Specific Failures

- **39.03%** of failures cite poor requirements gathering as the leading cause
- **21%** of companies say poorly managed requirements cause project failure
- Projects with documented specs are **50% more likely to succeed**
- Clear requirements before development: **97% more likely to succeed**
- Accurate requirements: **57% increase** in success rates

### The Communication Gap

- **57%** of failing projects cite communication breakdowns
- Domain experts speak business language
- Developers need technical specifications
- Traditional BA work is expensive ($70-150/hour)
- Manual spec creation takes days/weeks

## Market Drivers

### 1. Agile/DevOps Adoption
- Shift to iterative development requires continuous requirements refinement
- Traditional waterfall spec processes don't fit agile workflows
- Need for real-time, living documentation

### 2. Software Complexity
- Applications involve more integrations, stakeholders, regulatory requirements
- Edge cases multiply with complexity
- Manual documentation can't keep up

### 3. AI Capability Maturity
- LLMs now capable of nuanced conversation and knowledge extraction
- Claude, GPT-4 enable intelligent interviewing
- Cost of AI inference dropping rapidly

### 4. Developer Productivity Focus
- Coding AI tools (Copilot, Cursor) growing fastest: $4B (55% of AI spend)
- Companies investing heavily in developer efficiency
- Requirements is the next frontier after coding

### 5. Remote/Distributed Teams
- Async communication is standard
- Written specs more important than ever
- Need tools that work across time zones

## Key Market Trends

### AI in Enterprise Software
- Companies spent **$37B** on generative AI in 2025 (up from $11.5B in 2024)
- AI now captures **6%** of global SaaS market
- Copilots dominate with **86% share** ($7.2B)
